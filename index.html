Soonmin Hwang

The Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University

I am a postdoc researcher at the Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University, Pittsburgh, PA, United States. Before joining Carnegie Mellon, I worked for Tesla, Palo Alto, CA, United States, as a Senior Machine Learning Scientist.

I received my M.S and Ph.D. degrees in Electrical Engineering from KAIST, Korea in 2014 and 2019 respectively. During my graduate study, I worked at the Robotics and Computer Vision Lab under the supervision of Prof. In So Kweon. In 2016, I worked for Qualcomm Research, Vienna, Austria, as a Research Intern. I received Samsung HumanTech Paper Awards(Gold prize in 2017, honorable mention in 2018) and the 1st place at NVidia Korea Deep learning Contest in 2016. I received my B.S degree in Electronics and Computer Engineering from Hanyang University, Korea in 2012.

Publications

Gated Bidirectional Feature Pyramid Network for Accurate One-shot Detection

Sanghyun Woo, Soonmin Hwang, Ho-Deok Jang, In So Kweon
March 2019 In MVA
Despite recent advances in machine learning, it is still challenging to realize real-time and accurate detection in images. In this paper, we propose what is termed the gated bidirectional feature pyramid network (GBFPN), a simple and effective architecture that provides a significant improvement over the baseline model, StairNet.

Pedestrian Detection in the Wild: A Fault Tolerant Approach

Soonmin Hwang, Namil Kim, Yukyung Choi, In So Kweon
June 2018 preprint
Real-world challenges do occur from accidental situations that are not expected at the training phase. In this paper, we address a practical multispectral fusion issue as unexpected image contamination in day and night conditions.

KAIST Multispectral Recognition Dataset in Day and Night

Yukyung Choi, Namil Kim, Soonmin Hwang, Kibaek Park, Jae Shin Yoon, Kyunghwan An, In So Kweon
May 2018 In T-ITS
Over all days, we successfully captured 50km sequences of synchronized multiple sensors at 25Hz using a fully aligned visible and thermal device, high resolution stereo visible cameras, and high accuracy GPS/IMU inertial navigation system.

Thermal Image Enhancement using Convolution Neural Network

Yukyung Choi*, Namil Kim*, Soonmin Hwang*, In So Kweon
October 2016 In IROS
Existing thermal cameras have a relatively smaller resolution than RGB cameras that has trouble for fully utilizing the information in recognition tasks. We introduce Thermal Image Enhancement using Convolutional Neural Network (CNN), called in TEN, which directly learns an end-to-end mapping a single low resolution image to the desired high resolution image.
PDF Slides Video

StairNet: Top-Down Semantic Aggregation for Accurate One Shot Detection

Sanghyun Woo, Soonmin Hwang, In So Kweon
October 2015 In WACV
It is widely recognized that one-stage detectors have difficulty in detecting small objects while they are competitive with two-stage methods on large objects. We address this problem by introducing a feature combining module that spreads out the strong semantics in a top-down manner.

Multispectral Pedestrian Detection: Benchmark Dataset and Baselines

Soonmin Hwang, Jaesik Park, Namil Kim, Yukyung Choi, In So Kweon
June 2015 In CVPR
We propose a multispectral pedestrian dataset which provides well aligned color-thermal image pairs, captured by beam splitter-based special hardware. With this dataset, we introduce multispectral ACF, which is an extension of aggregated channel features (ACF) to simultaneously handle color-thermal image pairs.
